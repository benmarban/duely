Step by step

Pick your email provider

The names of the buttons change, but it's the same idea everywhere: find Forwarding in your mail settings and paste your Dayflow address.

Gmail (and Google Workspace)

Gmail makes you confirm the address with a code. Dayflow catches that code for you — you never have to go hunting for it.

  1. Copy your Dayflow address from the top of this page.
  2. Open Gmail on a computer. Click the ⚙ gear in the top-right, then See all settings. Gmail's mobile app can't set up forwarding — you need a browser, but a phone browser works.
  3. Go to the Forwarding and POP/IMAP tab and click Add a forwarding address.
  4. Paste your Dayflow address, click Next, then Proceed.
  5. Gmail sends a confirmation code to that address. It shows up inside Dayflow — open the app and look under Connections → Emails. It usually lands within a few seconds. Paste the code back into Gmail and hit Verify.
  6. Back on the Forwarding and POP/IMAP tab, select Forward a copy of incoming mail to and choose your Dayflow address.
  7. Leave the second dropdown on keep Gmail's copy in the Inbox, then click Save Changes at the very bottom.
Save Changes is easy to miss. It's a small link at the bottom of a long settings page. If forwarding doesn't seem to work, this is almost always why.
Don't want to forward everything? Use Filters and Blocked Addresses → Create a new filter, match the senders you care about (your coach, your school, your job), and choose Forward it to your Dayflow address.

Outlook, Hotmail & Microsoft 365

This covers outlook.com, hotmail.com, live.com, and most school accounts running on Microsoft 365.

  1. Copy your Dayflow address from the top of this page.
  2. Open Outlook on the web and click the ⚙ gear in the top-right corner.
  3. Choose Mail in the left column, then Forwarding.
  4. Tick Enable forwarding and paste your Dayflow address into the box.
  5. Tick Keep a copy of forwarded messages so mail still shows up in your own inbox. Without this, forwarded mail can skip your inbox entirely.
  6. Click Save.
On a school or work account? Many universities block forwarding to outside addresses, so the setting may be greyed out or your mail may bounce. If that happens, forward the specific emails you care about by hand, or ask your IT help desk whether external forwarding can be enabled.
Only want certain mail? Under Mail → Rules, create a rule with a condition (say, sender is your coach) and the action Redirect to your Dayflow address.

Yahoo Mail

Yahoo puts automatic forwarding behind its paid tier. If you're on the free plan, skip to the workaround below.

Heads up: automatic forwarding needs Yahoo Mail Plus, a paid subscription. Free accounts don't get the setting.
  1. Copy your Dayflow address from the top of this page.
  2. Open Yahoo Mail in a browser and click the ⚙ gear, then More Settings.
  3. Choose Mailboxes in the left column and click on your Yahoo address.
  4. Scroll to Forwarding, paste your Dayflow address, and click Verify.
  5. Yahoo sends a confirmation link to that address. Dayflow surfaces it for you — open the app and check Connections → Emails.
  6. Once verified, make sure forwarding is switched on and save.
On the free plan? Forward the emails that matter by hand — open one, hit Forward, send it to your Dayflow address. The dates get filed exactly the same way.

iCloud Mail

iCloud forwards to one address at a time, and the setting lives on iCloud.com rather than in the Mail app.

  1. Copy your Dayflow address from the top of this page.
  2. Go to icloud.com/mail in a browser and sign in.
  3. Click the ⚙ gear at the top of the mailbox list, then Settings (older layouts call it Preferences).
  4. Open the General tab and tick Forward my email to:.
  5. Paste your Dayflow address into the field.
  6. Leave Delete messages after forwarding unchecked — otherwise iCloud erases your mail once it's sent on. This one matters. Checked, your only copy leaves your inbox.
  7. Click Done.
Already forwarding somewhere else? iCloud only allows a single forwarding address. If yours is taken, set up a rule instead: Settings → Rules → Add a Rule, then Forward to your Dayflow address.

Any other email

Proton, Zoho, Fastmail, your school's webmail, whatever your internet provider gave you — forwarding is a standard feature and the shape of it is always the same.

  1. Copy your Dayflow address from the top of this page.
  2. Open your email in a browser and find Settings — usually a gear icon.
  3. Look for a section called Forwarding, Auto-forward, Filters, or Rules. Searching the web for "your provider + forward email automatically" gets you there fast.
  4. Paste your Dayflow address as the destination and save.
  5. If your provider emails a confirmation code or link, look for it inside Dayflow under Connections → Emails — that mail arrives at your Dayflow address, not your inbox.
  6. Keep any "save a copy in my inbox" option turned on.
No forwarding setting anywhere? You can always forward individual emails by hand. Open the message, tap Forward, and send it to your Dayflow address — same result, just one email at a time.
What happens to your mail

Dayflow is looking for dates. That's all.

Forwarding your inbox is a real thing to trust someone with, so here's exactly what happens on the other end.

What it does

  • Reads each email for a date, a time, and a title
  • Files what it finds onto your timeline
  • Shows you the source email behind every event
  • Catches forwarding confirmation codes so you don't have to

What it doesn't

  • Sell or share your mail with anyone
  • Keep emails that hold no date — they're dropped
  • Reply, send, or act on anything in your name
  • Touch your inbox — forwarding is one-way, and copies stay with you
Your address is a secret. Anyone who has it can put events on your timeline. Treat it like a password — don't post it, and don't hand it out.
Loose ends

Common questions

How long until my first email shows up?

Seconds. Forward one email by hand as a test — if it has a date in it, the event appears on your timeline almost immediately. If nothing shows up after a minute, the email probably didn't contain a date Dayflow recognized.

Will my inbox get emptied out?

No. Forwarding sends a copy. The original stays right where it is, as long as you leave options like Gmail's "keep Gmail's copy in the Inbox" or iCloud's "delete messages after forwarding" set the way this guide describes.

Can I link more than one inbox?

Yes — point your school email, your personal email, and your work email at the same Dayflow address. Everything lands in one timeline. Free accounts can link one inbox; Pro removes the limit.

Gmail never sent me the confirmation code.

The code goes to your Dayflow address, not your inbox, which is why it feels like it vanished. Open the app and look under Connections → Emails — Dayflow catches the code and shows it there. If it's still missing, double-check you pasted the address exactly, with no spaces on either end.

What about promos, receipts, and newsletters?

Ignored. Dayflow only keeps an email if it can find a real date and event in it. Everything else is thrown away rather than stored.

How do I turn this off?

Go back to the same forwarding setting in your email provider and switch it off. Dayflow stops receiving anything the moment you do — there's nothing to cancel on our end.
